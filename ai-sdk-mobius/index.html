AI INTERACTION DESIGN

Transforming a neglected AI SKD web demo into a vital sales tool for a Computer Vision startup (acquired by Dropbox)

Increased Sales Usage to 100%

CHALLENGE

Despite having powerful computer vision technology, the company’s public web demo was feature-heavy rather than industry-oriented & benefit-driven, leading the sales team to avoid using it in live customer meetings.

MY TASK

Led the end-to-end redesign of the Mobius Labs SDK web demo to align with industry-specific needs. Bridged Product and Sales by defining industry personas, creating a scalable design system, and delivering an interactive demo product that enabled the sales team to showcase the real-time capabilities of Mobius AI during client pitches.

TASK TIMEFRAME

5 months

KEY FOCUS AREAS

  • Internal UX auditing & "New Hire" usability testing
  • Sales team interviews & Industry Persona definition
  • Design System creation (MUI + Figma + Storybook)
  • High-fidelity prototyping

ALL RESULTS

  • Increased demo usage in sales meetings by 95%
  • Accelerated design production speed by implementing a unified MUI-based Design System
  • Established a "new hire testing loop" agreement with HR to secure continuous, free user feedback

CORE RESULT

The redesigned demo product drove a 95% increase in usage during sales meetings.

OVERVIEW OF MY DESIGN PROCESS

Step 1 - Internal UX review workshop:

Conducted "labor illusion" experiments and usability tests with new employees during my first two weeks, identifying that users were overwhelmed by technical jargon and confusing onboarding.

Step 2 - Sales team interviews & deal analysis:

Interviewed 4 experts most involved in sales process (sales specialists, CEO and tech lead) and analyzed 30+ HubSpot deals to understand the disconnect. Discovered that while the product demo sold "features," clients were buying "solutions" specific to their industry.

Step 3 - Defining industry personas:

Synthesized research into 4 key industry personas (Content Licensing, Internet-to-Print, Broadcasting, Marketing) to ensure the new design spoke the language of the decision-makers, not just developers.

Step 4 - Building the infrastructure:

Created a comprehensive Design System (Figma library with MUI-based components linked to Storybook) to eliminate messy custom add-ons and inconsistent fonts, streamlining the implementation process.

Step 5 - Strategic redesign & vertical prototyping:

Executed the “industry-oriented” redesign with improved contrast and clear, step-by-step processes.

Step 6 - Implementation & sales feedback:

Launched the redesigned demo and gathered feedback 12 months later, confirming the tool now provides the necessary credibility to transition prospects into Proof of Concepts (POCs).
